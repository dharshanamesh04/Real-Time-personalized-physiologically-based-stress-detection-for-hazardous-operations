Why Stress Matters in Hazardous Work

In hazardous environments, stress directly impacts operational safety. Physiological signals provide objective, measurable indicators of stress levels unlike subjective assessments.

Critical Concerns:

Poor decision-making under pressure.

Slowed reaction times to emergencies.

Increased safety incident probability.

Difficulty recovering from mental fatigue.

05 // Biometric Indicators

Key Physiological Parameters

Our system monitors four core physiological signals to triangulate stress levels with high precision.

Parameter What It Measures Normal Resting Range Stress Indicator Range Why It Matters
❤️ Heart Rate (HR) Beats per minute (BPM) 60 – 100 BPM 100+ BPM at rest Elevated heart rate without physical activity indicates autonomic stress activation.
💓 Heart Rate Variability (HRV) Variation between beats (ms) 30 – 70 ms Below 20–30 ms Low HRV suggests the body is in "fight-or-flight" mode with reduced recovery capacity.
💧 Electrodermal Activity (EDA) Skin conductance (µS) 1 – 10 µS Sudden spikes Responds rapidly to emotional or cognitive load through sweat gland activation.
🌬️ Respiration Rate Breaths per minute 12 – 20 breaths/min 20+ breaths/min Rapid, shallow breathing reduces oxygen efficiency and signals acute stress levels.

07 // Technical Core

The Engineering Behind the Intelligence

Our system redefines the standard for safety by addressing the core limitation of modern biometrics: Generalization.

By implementing a Personalized Time-Series Machine Learning Framework, we move beyond "one-size-fits-all" models. Physiological signals—including Heart Rate, Blood Pressure, EDA, and Respiration—are continuously streamed through a sophisticated Sliding Window Feature Extraction pipeline.

This process derives critical statistical parameters—such as mean, slope, and standard deviation—to capture the subtle temporal trends that precede a stress-related incident.

System Architecture

  • Classifier: User-specific Random Forest
  • Logic: Adaptive Historical Pattern Recognition
  • Frontend: Flask-based Real-time Dashboard
  • Output: 3-Tier Discrete Stress Classification
